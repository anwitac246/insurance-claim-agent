## SECTION 2: TYPES OF COVERAGE OFFERED

### 2.1 Comprehensive Coverage (Package Policy)
Covers Own Damage (OD) to the insured vehicle AND Third-Party Liability (TP).

**OD Coverage Includes:**
- Accidental damage due to collision, overturning, or falling objects
- Fire, explosion, self-ignition, or lightning
- Burglary, theft, or housebreaking
- Riot, strike, malicious act, or terrorist activity
- Natural calamities: flood, inundation, cyclone, hailstorm, earthquake, landslide, rockslide
- Damage while in transit by road, rail, inland waterway, lift, elevator, or air

**Third-Party Liability Includes:**
- Bodily injury or death of a third party
- Property damage to a third party (capped at ₹7.5 Lakhs as per IRDAI 2026 tariff)

### 2.2 Third-Party Only (TP) Policy
- Mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988
- Does NOT cover Own Damage to insured vehicle
- Covers unlimited liability for third-party death/injury
- Covers third-party property damage up to ₹7.5 Lakhs

### 2.3 Own Damage (OD) Standalone Policy
- Applicable for vehicles with a separate, valid TP policy
- Covers all OD perils listed in Section 2.1
- Must be linked to an active TP policy number at time of claim

## SECTION 3: INSURED DECLARED VALUE (IDV) RULES

### 3.1 IDV Calculation
IDV = (Manufacturer's Listed Selling Price - Depreciation) + Value of Accessories not included in MSP

**Depreciation Schedule (as per IRDAI 2026):**
| Vehicle Age | Depreciation % |
|---|---|
| Not exceeding 6 months | 5% |
| 6 months to 1 year | 15% |
| 1 year to 2 years | 20% |
| 2 years to 3 years | 30% |
| 3 years to 4 years | 40% |
| 4 years to 5 years | 50% |
| Beyond 5 years | Mutually agreed IDV |

### 3.2 IDV as Maximum Liability
- The maximum claim payout under OD shall NEVER exceed the IDV stated on the Policy Schedule.
- Total Loss (Constructive Total Loss): If repair cost exceeds 75% of IDV, the vehicle is declared a Total Loss, and the full IDV minus salvage value is paid.

### 3.3 Underinsurance Penalty
- If the policyholder has declared an IDV lower than the market value to reduce premiums, claims are settled on a pro-rata basis:
- Claim Payout = (Declared IDV / Actual Market IDV) × Assessed Loss Amount

## SECTION 4: STANDARD EXCLUSIONS (WHAT IS NOT COVERED)

The following situations render a claim INVALID and must be flagged for REJECTION:

### 4.1 Absolute Exclusions (No Exceptions)
- **E-001:** Driving under the influence of alcohol (BAC > 0.03%) or narcotics — REJECT IMMEDIATELY
- **E-002:** Driving without a valid, appropriate-class Driver's License (expired, fake, or wrong class)
- **E-003:** Vehicle used for a purpose not covered by the policy (e.g., private vehicle used for paid taxi service/hire without commercial endorsement)
- **E-004:** Damage caused by willful negligence, intentional act, or racing
- **E-005:** Consequential loss (e.g., loss of income, depreciation of parts not replaced)
- **E-006:** Wear and tear, mechanical/electrical breakdown not caused by an accident
- **E-007:** Nuclear, biological, or chemical warfare damage

### 4.2 Conditional Exclusions (Claimable with Proof)
- **E-008:** Damage outside declared geographical area — claimable only with valid transit documents
- **E-009:** Accessories and modifications not declared at policy inception — NOT claimable unless added via endorsement
- **E-010:** Damage to tyres and tubes — Only claimable if vehicle also sustained other body damage in the same incident (50% of tyre cost covered)

## SECTION 5: POLICY VALIDITY CONDITIONS

- The policy must be ACTIVE (not expired) on the DATE OF ACCIDENT.
- Policy renewal within 90 days of expiry with no break is considered continuous coverage (grace for TP; not for OD).
- If the policy has lapsed (break > 90 days), no claim is admissible until fresh policy inception + 30-day cooling-off period passes.
- The vehicle's Registration Certificate (RC) must match the policy exactly: same engine number, chassis number, and registration number.

---

## SECTION 6: NO-CLAIM BONUS (NCB) RULES

- NCB is awarded to the policyholder (not the vehicle) for each claim-free year.
- If a claim is filed and approved, NCB resets to 0% for the next renewal.
- NCB Scale: 0% (Yr 0) → 20% (Yr 1) → 25% (Yr 2) → 35% (Yr 3) → 45% (Yr 4) → 50% (Yr 5+)
- Filing a fraudulent claim results in permanent NCB forfeiture and policy blacklisting.

## SECTION 7: AGENT AI DECISION RULES

For the AI claim processing system, the following hard rules apply to every claim:

**RULE-M-001:** ANY claim with a missing RC or DL must be flagged as INCOMPLETE. Do not proceed to coverage or fraud check.
**RULE-M-002:** Policy expiry date must be verified BEFORE any coverage check. An expired policy → AUTO-REJECT.
**RULE-M-003:** Engine/Chassis number on RC must EXACTLY MATCH the policy document. A mismatch → FLAG FOR HUMAN REVIEW.
**RULE-M-004:** IDV on the claim estimate must be checked against the Policy Schedule IDV. If estimate > IDV, cap the payout at IDV.
**RULE-M-005:** All claims above ₹1,00,000 (1 Lakh) must have KYC documents (Aadhaar/PAN) attached.
**RULE-M-006:** All claims involving fire, theft, major accident, or third-party injury must have an FIR attached. Missing FIR → HOLD CLAIM.
**RULE-M-007:** If fraud score from Fraud Agent exceeds 0.75 (75%), the claim must be AUTO-REJECTED and flagged for SIU (Special Investigation Unit).
